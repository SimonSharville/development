MAKER SPACES

How to enhance your modelmaking capability, increase productivity and cut costs.

I can help you:

  • Assess your relationship with models and making

  • Understand the creative and financial advantages of an In-House facility

  • Identify options for your workshop potential

  • Understand the budget needed for what you want

  • Source all the equipment and specialist suppliers

  • Design a great looking and ergonomic maker space/workshop and staff it

  • Design a Photography area, specify equipment and teach you how to use it.

    • I will guide you through all of the above questions tailoring my input to your needs and enabling your practice to create everything from fast everyday sketches through diagrammatic concept models to fully detailed competition entries or marketing/exhibition models. Consultancy is available in individual stages: Initial assessment, design and finally project management through the build.

  • The physical model is still the most powerful magnet in any presentation or pitch

  • Models win work and contribute to the efficiency of the design and construction process

  • Combine new tech with professional modelmakers to fully enhance and exploit it and you get hand built models much faster

  • Models can be charged to the client. From a two day laser cut diagrammatic acrylic concept model with the dynamic energy of a hand sketch to the sophistication of a fully detailed colour marketing model

  • In all my time heading an In-house modelshop it always at least ‘washed its face’ financially and often made profit

  • Making is on the up and other practices of a certain size are considering their capability

  • You would recoup the costs currently spent using external companies for models, laser cutting or 3D printing.

  • Relationships between elements and to context are explored whilst making, informing and short-cutting the design process making it more efficient

  • Issues are considered to focus the viewer on the broader architectural/design vision. Ideas and concepts are given emphasis and literal interpretation can be filtered rather than showing the nuts and bolts of buildings not yet designed

  • Symbiotic relationships develop between architects/designers and modelmakers who develop the ability to work collaboratively, with evolving information

  • The haptic feedback from materials, or spatial interactions from life-size mock ups are invaluable. The materiality of a timber model with its beauty, warmth and charm instantly engages both public and professional with the abstraction of wood understood by all

  • As schemes evolve, In-House model maker skills for deconstruction, remodelling and reassembly are valuable in making modifications so that a model can remain part of the design process and not be left behind as a fixed moment in time

  • In-house styles can be developed to broadcast a practices design style and promote their passion for well-designed and well-crafted buildings

  • A modelmaking workshop acts a cultural hub across all teams, softening professional boundaries, supporting and nurturing collaboration and team spirit whilst engendering a sense of fun at work
